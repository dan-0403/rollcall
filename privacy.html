1. Introduction

RollCall is an attendance management service used to record and manage check-ins and check-outs for students and/or employees. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, how we protect it, and the choices you have about your personal information.

2. Data We Collect

We may collect the following types of information when users interact with the System:

  • Identity & Profile Data: name, student/employee ID, class/department, profile photo (if provided).
  • Attendance Records: timestamps for time-in and time-out, location or terminal ID where check-in occurred, device identifier (when applicable).
  • Contact Information: email address and phone number (used for notifications or account recovery).
  • Device & Usage Data: browser, IP address, device type, log files and usage analytics needed to operate and improve the System.
  • Optional Data: notes, custom fields, or other information you or your organization chooses to provide.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use collected information for purposes including but not limited to:

  • Recording, displaying and reporting attendance and punctuality.
  • Sending alerts, reminders, and notifications (e.g., absence alerts).
  • Providing and maintaining the System, troubleshooting issues, and improving features.
  • Generating analytics and reports for administrators (aggregated or de-identified where possible).
  • Complying with legal obligations and enforcing our terms of service.

4. Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell personal data. We may share data in these limited circumstances:

  • With your organization / administrators: administrators who manage attendance will have access to attendance data and related reports.
  • Service Providers: vendors who process data on our behalf (hosting, analytics, email delivery). They are contractually required to protect your data.
  • Legal Requirements: if required by law or to protect rights, safety, or property.

When we share data, we require third parties to maintain safeguards and limit use only to purposes we authorize.

5. Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information against accidental loss, unauthorized access, or disclosure. These measures may include encryption in transit, access controls, and regular security assessments.

However, no system is completely secure; if you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately.

6. Data Retention

We retain attendance data and account information for as long as necessary to provide the service, fulfill legitimate business purposes (such as reporting and compliance), or until your organization requests deletion. Specific retention periods may be configured by your organization.

7. Your Rights & Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction and our relationship (e.g., whether you are a user, parent, or administrator), you may have rights to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Request deletion of your account or personal data (subject to legal and administrative retention needs).
  • Opt out of non-essential communications (e.g., marketing emails if applicable).

To exercise these rights, contact the data controller listed below. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling requests.

8. Children's Privacy

Our System may be used in educational settings. If you are a parent or guardian and you believe we have collected personal information about a child without appropriate consent, please contact us so we can take action.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will post the updated policy and update the effective date. Please review this page periodically.
